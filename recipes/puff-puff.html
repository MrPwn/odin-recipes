Puff Puff

Description

Nigerian puff puff recipe! Looking for the best? Look no further than this easy, perfect recipe that will give you soft, delicious, golden brown, well-rounded, deep-fried dough that is similar to Ghanaian bofrot. Learn how to make puff puff in simple steps.

Ingredients

These amounts yield 10 servings
  • 400g self raising flour
  • 165g sugar
  • 7g fast action dried yeast
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 360-380 ml warm water
  • vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Add flour, sugar, nutmeg, salt, and yeast to a bowl. Mix well till the dry ingredients are combined.
  2. Add warm water (not hot) and mix till it comes together.
  3. Cover the bowl with clingfilm and put in a warm place to rise for 90-120 minutes
  4. Add oil to a deep pan (Let the oil be at least 3 inches deep) and heat.When the oil is hot at about 160C - 180C, use your hand to scoop the batter and gently drop in the oil. You can also use an ice cream scoop to scoop batter into the oil. Drop the batter into the oil till the pot is crowded.
  5. When one side is fried to a golden brown, flip the balls to the other side and fry till golden brown. Take out of oil and drain on kitchen towel covered plate.
  6. Repeat steps 4 and 5 until all the dough is used up
  7. Serve and enjoy. It is not traditionally dusted with sweetener however you can dust with granulated or icing sugar like you would doughnuts.
